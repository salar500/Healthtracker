Why BMI Changes With Age

BMI doesn’t stay the same throughout life. As the body ages, natural physical changes can affect how BMI is interpreted.

1. Muscle Loss (Sarcopenia)

As you age, muscle mass gradually declines. This raises body fat percentage even if your weight or BMI remains the same.

2. Slower Metabolism

Older adults burn fewer calories at rest, making weight gain more likely even with similar eating habits.

3. Hormonal Changes

Changes in hormones such as estrogen, testosterone, and growth hormone influence appetite, energy levels, and fat storage.

4. Reduced Bone Density

Bone mass decreases with age, which can slightly lower body weight and affect the accuracy of BMI calculations.

Important: BMI becomes less accurate for older adults. Use it only as a general guideline—not a diagnosis.
