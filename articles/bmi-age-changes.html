Why BMI Changes With Age

BMI is influenced not only by height and weight but also by natural aging processes.

1. Muscle Loss (Sarcopenia)

As you age, muscle mass decreases, which can raise body fat percentage even if BMI stays constant.

2. Metabolism Slows Down

Older adults burn fewer calories at rest, making weight gain easier.

3. Hormonal Changes

Shifts in hormones affect appetite, fat storage, and energy levels.

4. Bone Density Reduces

Lower bone mass can slightly reduce weight, affecting BMI calculations.

Important: BMI is less accurate for older adults—use it as a guide, not a diagnosis.
