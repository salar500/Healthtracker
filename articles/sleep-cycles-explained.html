Sleep Cycles Explained: How Your Body Sleeps

Every night, your body goes through several sleep cycles — each one vital for energy, memory, and overall health. Understanding these stages helps you sleep better and use the Sleep Calculator more effectively.

What Is a Sleep Cycle?

A full sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and repeats 4–6 times per night. Each cycle includes multiple stages that shift between light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep.

Stage 1: Light Sleep

This is the transition phase — your heart rate slows, muscles relax, and your body begins shutting down. It’s easy to wake up during this stage.

Stage 2: Deeper Light Sleep

Stage 2 makes up the largest part of the sleep cycle. Your brain slows down, and your body temperature drops.

Stage 3: Deep Sleep

This is your body’s physical restoration phase. Deep sleep helps:

  • boost immune system
  • repair muscles
  • restore energy

Stage 4: REM Sleep

REM (Rapid Eye Movement) is when most dreaming happens. This stage is essential for:

  • learning
  • memory
  • emotional balance

Why Sleep Cycles Matter

Waking up in the middle of deep sleep makes you feel tired and foggy. Waking up at the end of a cycle helps you feel refreshed — that’s why the HealthTracker Sleep Calculator uses 90-minute intervals.

How to Improve Your Sleep Cycles

  • Go to bed at consistent times.
  • Avoid screens 1 hour before sleep.
  • Keep your room dark and cool.
  • Avoid caffeine late in the day.
