How Much Water Should You Drink Daily?

Staying hydrated is essential for energy, focus, digestion, skin health, and overall wellness. But how much water do you actually need each day?

The General Rule

A simple guideline is to drink 35 ml of water per kilogram of body weight. This means:

  • 50 kg person → 1.7 L/day
  • 60 kg person → 2.1 L/day
  • 75 kg person → 2.6 L/day
  • 90 kg person → 3.1 L/day

For exact numbers based on your activity and climate, use our Hydration Calculator.

Factors That Change Your Water Needs

  • Hot weather: You lose more water through sweat.
  • Exercise: Activity increases fluid loss.
  • High-sodium diets: Can increase thirst.
  • Illness: Fever and vomiting raise water needs.

Conclusion

Drinking enough water is one of the simplest ways to support your health. Start by calculating your daily needs and adjust based on climate and activity.
