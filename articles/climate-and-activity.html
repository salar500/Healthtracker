How Climate & Activity Affect Your Water Needs

Your body’s hydration needs change depending on how active you are and the environment around you. Hot weather and physical effort both increase water loss.

1. Hot Climate

In hot or humid conditions, your body sweats more to cool itself, causing faster dehydration.

  • Increase intake by 20–40% in hot environments.
  • Drink more during outdoor activities.

2. Exercise & Activity

Exercise increases water loss through sweat. Even light workouts require extra hydration.

  • Add 300–700 ml of water per 30 minutes of exercise.
  • Drink before, during, and after workouts.
