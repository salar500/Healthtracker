Sleep Cycles Explained: How Your Body Sleeps

Your body moves through different sleep stages every night — each one important for memory, recovery, and energy. Understanding how sleep cycles work can dramatically improve your overall sleep quality.

What Is a Sleep Cycle?

A complete sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes, repeating 4–6 times each night. These cycles move between light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep.

Stage 1: Light Sleep

This is the transition phase. Heart rate slows, muscles relax, and it is easy to wake up from this stage.

Stage 2: Deeper Light Sleep

Stage 2 is the largest portion of your total sleep. Brain waves slow, and body temperature drops.

Stage 3: Deep Sleep

Deep sleep is essential for physical restoration:

  • immune system support
  • muscle and tissue repair
  • energy restoration

Stage 4: REM Sleep

REM sleep is when most dreams occur. It is vital for:

  • learning
  • memory formation
  • emotional regulation

Why Sleep Cycles Matter

Waking during deep sleep causes morning grogginess. Waking at the *end* of a cycle helps you feel more refreshed — this is exactly how the Sleep Calculator determines ideal wake times.

How to Improve Your Sleep Cycles

  • Go to bed at the same time each night.
  • Avoid screens 1 hour before sleeping.
  • Keep your room cool, dark, and quiet.
  • Limit caffeine in the afternoon and evening.
