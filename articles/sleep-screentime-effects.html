How Screen Time Affects Your Sleep

Using phones, laptops, or TVs before bed can significantly reduce sleep quality. Here’s how nighttime screen use disrupts your sleep — and simple ways to fix it.

1. Blue Light Blocks Melatonin Production

Screens emit blue light that suppresses melatonin — the natural hormone that makes you feel sleepy. Less melatonin = harder to fall asleep and poorer sleep quality.

2. Mental Stimulation Keeps the Brain Awake

Social media, gaming, and videos keep your brain active when it should be winding down. This delays your “sleep mode” and makes falling asleep slower.

3. Shorter and Lighter Sleep Cycles

Studies show that late-night screen use leads to:

  • more frequent awakenings
  • reduced REM sleep
  • lighter and less restorative sleep

4. Best Practices to Reduce Screen Impact

  • Stop using screens at least 1 hour before bed.
  • Turn on blue light filter / night mode on your devices.
  • Dim room lighting before bedtime.
  • Keep your room cool, dark, and quiet to improve sleep quality.
