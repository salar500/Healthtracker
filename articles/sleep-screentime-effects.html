How Screen Time Affects Your Sleep

Using phones, laptops, or TVs before bed can reduce sleep quality significantly. Here’s how screen time disrupts your sleep — and how to fix it.

1. Blue Light Blocks Melatonin Production

The blue light from screens suppresses melatonin, the hormone your body uses to feel sleepy.

2. Mental Stimulation Keeps the Brain Awake

Social media, gaming, and videos keep your mind alert — not ideal before bedtime.

3. Shorter and Lighter Sleep Cycles

Studies show that late-night screen use leads to more awakenings and shorter REM sleep.

4. Best Practices to Reduce Screen Impact

  • Stop using screens **at least 1 hour before bed**.
  • Enable **blue light filter / night mode** on your devices.
  • Keep your room dark and cool for better sleep.

Reducing screen time at night can dramatically improve the quality of your rest.
