Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is a useful quick measurement, it has several important limitations that prevent it from showing a full picture of a person’s health.

1. BMI Does Not Measure Body Fat

People with high muscle mass (like athletes) may be classified as “overweight” even with very low body fat.

2. BMI Ignores Fat Distribution

BMI cannot show where fat is stored — belly fat is more dangerous than fat in other areas.

3. Less Accurate for Certain Groups

Children, older adults, bodybuilders, and pregnant individuals often get misleading BMI results.

4. BMI Is Only One Health Indicator

A full evaluation requires more data — body fat %, waist size, activity level, and overall fitness.
